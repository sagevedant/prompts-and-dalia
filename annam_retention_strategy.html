Subscription Architecture
Revenue Layer
Launch Timing ‚Äî Exact Reasoning

Launch Subscription at Order #1. Not After 100 Orders.

  • Reason 1: Purchase intent is highest at the moment of first buy ‚Äî conversion to subscription drops sharply after 72 hours
  • Reason 2: First 100 customers are your most curious, most forgiving cohort ‚Äî perfect to test sub mechanics without backlash
  • Reason 3: "Founding Subscriber" framing only works pre-scale ‚Äî it loses power at 500+ customers
  • Caveat: Do NOT make subscription default. Present it as the smarter choice with clear benefit, let them opt in.
Cadence Decision

Bi-Weekly (Every 14 Days). Not Weekly. Not Monthly.

  • Weekly = 1 pack/week = only 5 bowls. Customer runs out mid-week. Anxiety. Churns.
  • Monthly = too long to feel the habit. Feels like a bulk order, not a ritual.
  • Bi-weekly = 2 packs arrive every 14 days = ~10 bowls = exactly right for 5x/week breakfast eater with room for variety
  • Bi-weekly also means 26 touchpoints/year vs 12 with monthly ‚Äî more brand contact without subscription fatigue

The Anti-Churn Playbook
Defence Layer
The Moment Most Food Subscriptions Lose Customers

Order #2. Not #5, Not #10. Order #2.

  • Order #1 is novelty. Order #2 is a decision: "Is this actually part of my life?"
  • Most brands celebrate the first purchase and go quiet ‚Äî fatal error
  • Between D7 and D14, customers are mentally evaluating: shelf space, routine fit, value vs alternatives
  • Churn trigger: forgetting Annam existed when it's time to reorder. No pack = no reminder = no habit
  • Fix: Bi-weekly subscription removes this decision entirely. Auto-delivery = auto-habit.
When Someone Tries to Cancel

Don't Immediately Offer a Discount. Ask First.

  • Step 1: Reply within 4 hours (WhatsApp)
  • Step 2: "Before we do that ‚Äî quick question. Is it the price, the taste, or just the timing isn't right now?"
  • Step 3 (Price): Offer 1 month at ‚Çπ125/pack (Committed rate)
  • Step 3 (Taste): "We have a variant coming. Can we send you a sample before you leave?"
  • Step 3 (Timing): Offer Pause (see below)
  • Never offer a discount without understanding why they're leaving ‚Äî you'll train customers to fake-cancel for discounts
Pause Instead of Cancel

Exact Offer ‚Äî Reply PAUSE to WhatsApp

  • Options offered: "Pause for 2 weeks / Pause for 1 month / Skip next delivery"
  • Framing: "Your founding rate stays locked. Just come back when you're ready."
  • Auto-resume: Subscription resumes after pause period with zero action needed from customer
  • Pause reminder: 3 days before resume ‚Äî "Your Annam is coming back on [date]. Reply EXTEND if you need more time."
  • Expected outcome: 55‚Äì65% of those who pause resume vs. ~10% who return after cancelling
